Lesson 1 — Introduction to Databases & Importance of Structuring: The Attendance Problem

Lesson 1 Objectives

  • Define what a Database is
  • Explain the idea of Structured Data with examples
  • Recognize the importance of structuring data in school record-keeping

Intro — Story Outline

In a school, a teacher struggles with checking the students' attendance. Sometimes the list goes missing, and sometimes there are too many entries. The principal often scolds the teacher because the records are messy.

In this short story, we’ll see how the principal himself came up with a better way to manage data — which they called a DATABASE.

Database & Structured Data Summary

Database: A central place to store information, making it easy to organize, manage, and retrieve data efficiently.

Structured Data: Data organized in a clear and consistent format to avoid confusion, improve accuracy, and simplify access.

This ensures that information is reliable, complete, and easy to find.
